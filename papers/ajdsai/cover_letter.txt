Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Quantum Sentiment
Classification with DisCoCat: A Controlled Empirical Study on a Short
Phrase SST-2 Subset" for consideration as a Research Paper in the
inaugural issues of the Asian Journal of Data Science and Artificial
Intelligence (AJDSAI).

The paper investigates the practical performance of Quantum Natural
Language Processing (QNLP) using the Distributional Compositional
Categorical (DisCoCat) framework. We report a controlled experimental
grid of 60 quantum training runs on the public Stanford Sentiment
Treebank benchmark, compare against three classical baselines (LR with
TF-IDF, LR with GloVe embeddings, and a Bidirectional LSTM), and
analyse differences using paired McNemar tests.

We position the manuscript as a reproducible methodology
contribution to data science, with the following reasons for AJDSAI
fit:

1. Topical alignment: The paper sits at the intersection of data
   science and artificial intelligence, with a clear
   interdisciplinary character (computational linguistics, category
   theory, quantum information). We are deliberate about scope: the
   experiments are confined to a short-phrase subset of SST-2
   trained on a noiseless CPU simulator, and the paper does not
   claim transfer to finance applications, NISQ hardware, or
   non-English languages without further study.

2. Methodological transparency: We release a fully reproducible
   pipeline (data preparation, 60-run experimental grid, evaluation,
   paired McNemar testing with Holm--Bonferroni adjustment over a
   pre-specified family of m = 16 comparisons) at
   https://github.com/hublinhdn/nlp_quantum_hub, and document a
   concrete infrastructure-level reproducibility issue we
   encountered in the QNLP toolchain (lambeq 0.5 BobcatParser
   endpoint), contributing to the broader reproducibility discussion
   in applied AI.

3. Original empirical findings: We report two methodologically
   informative results that, to the best of our knowledge, have not
   previously been documented in the QNLP literature on a public
   benchmark: a representational saturation phenomenon of the
   Spiders reader, and a reproducible barren-plateau-like collapse
   of the Cups + IQP family at depth two.

4. Regional scope bridge: One of the open directions discussed in
   the paper is the extension of the DisCoCat pipeline to
   non-English short-phrase sentiment benchmarks (for example a
   Vietnamese short-phrase benchmark, which to our knowledge does
   not yet exist as a public resource), which we believe is a
   natural fit with the regional data-science remit of the journal.

The manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
consideration elsewhere. All authors have read and approved the
submitted version and agree to its submission to AJDSAI.
