Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Parameter-Efficient
Quantum Sentiment Classification: A Controlled Empirical Study of
DisCoCat on SST-2" for consideration as a Research Paper in the
inaugural issues of the Asian Journal of Data Science and Artificial
Intelligence (AJDSAI).

The paper investigates the practical performance of Quantum Natural
Language Processing (QNLP) using the Distributional Compositional
Categorical (DisCoCat) framework. We report a controlled experimental
grid of 60 quantum training runs on the public Stanford Sentiment
Treebank benchmark, compare against three classical baselines (LR with
TF-IDF, LR with GloVe embeddings, and a Bidirectional LSTM), and
analyse differences using paired McNemar tests.

We believe the manuscript is a natural fit for AJDSAI for the
following reasons:

1. Topical alignment: The paper sits squarely in the intersection of
   data science and artificial intelligence, with a clear
   interdisciplinary character (computational linguistics, category
   theory, quantum information).

2. Practical relevance: The headline result --- a quantum model that
   ties a comparable BiLSTM with 28 times fewer trainable parameters
   --- has immediate practical implications for organisations seeking
   to deploy machine learning on resource-constrained or near-term
   quantum hardware, including potential applications in
   customer-feedback analytics, social-media monitoring and
   product-review classification.

3. Methodological transparency: We release a fully reproducible
   pipeline (data preparation, model training, evaluation, statistical
   testing) and document a concrete reproducibility issue we
   encountered in the QNLP toolchain, contributing to the broader
   reproducibility discussion in applied AI.

4. Original empirical findings: We report two methodologically
   informative results that, to the best of our knowledge, have not
   previously been documented in the QNLP literature on a public
   benchmark: a representational saturation phenomenon of the Spiders
   reader, and a reproducible barren-plateau collapse of the
   Cups + IQP family at depth two.

The manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
consideration elsewhere. All authors have read and approved the
submitted version and agree to its submission to AJDSAI.
